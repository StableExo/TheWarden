# 26 USC §6103 — Confidentiality and Disclosure of Returns and Return Information
**Title:** IRS Confidentiality + Authorized Disclosures to Law Enforcement

## Why This Matters for the Red Web

§6103 is a **dual-purpose statute**:
1. **Protects YOUR identity** — IRS keeps your information confidential
2. **Enables MULTIPLIER EFFECT** — IRS can share your tip with DOJ, OFAC, FinCEN, and state AGs

The key provision is **§6103(k)(13)** — added by the AML Whistleblower Improvement Act — which explicitly authorizes IRS to share whistleblower information with Treasury/FinCEN/OFAC.

This means your **FinCEN tip (filed today)** + **IRS Form 211** can legally feed the same intelligence to multiple agencies simultaneously, without exposing your identity.

---

## §6103(a) — General Rule: Returns Are Confidential

> Returns and return information shall be confidential, and except as authorized by this title, no officer or employee of the United States or any State shall disclose any return or return information obtained by him in any manner in connection with his service as such an officer or employee.

**Implication:** The IRS cannot reveal who submitted Form 211 or what was contained in it — without your consent or a court order.

---

## §6103(h) — Disclosure to DOJ ← CRITICAL

IRS can disclose return information to the Department of Justice **for tax administration purposes**, including:

- Criminal investigations under Title 18 (wire fraud, money laundering, CFAA)
- Civil tax litigation
- DOJ National Fraud Enforcement Division

**Implication for Red Web:** Your Form 211 tip, once received by IRS, can be routed directly to DOJ's criminal division for prosecution of 18 USC §1956 (money laundering) and wire fraud.

---

## §6103(k)(6) — Disclosure for Investigations of Tax-Related Crimes

IRS can disclose return information to federal law enforcement agencies investigating:
- Money laundering (18 USC §1956)
- Tax evasion (26 USC §7201)
- Financial crimes generally

---

## §6103(k)(13) — Disclosure to FinCEN / AML Whistleblower Program ← NEW

Added by the AML Whistleblower Improvement Act. Authorizes Treasury/IRS to:
- Share information with FinCEN
- Coordinate with OFAC on sanctions violations
- Feed data to the AML Whistleblower Program cross-referencing system

**Implication:** Your IRS Form 211 and FinCEN TCR are now legally linked. One feeds the other without double-jeopardy concerns. Both programs can act simultaneously on the same information, and both can pay separate awards.

---

## §6103(n) — Working With Whistleblowers Directly

IRS can share limited return information WITH the whistleblower during the investigation to help them assist the case. This is the authority the IRS Whistleblower Office uses when they "work closely with whistleblowers" (mentioned in their operating plan).

**Implication:** If IRS opens a formal case on the Red Web, they may contact you for additional intelligence — and can share what they find with you under this provision.
