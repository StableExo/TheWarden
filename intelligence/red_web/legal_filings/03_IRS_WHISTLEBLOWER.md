## ⚖️ Governing Law (from IRS.gov screenshots — GL-L93)

### Internal Revenue Code (IRC)
- **IRC §7623** — Authorization to pay awards. Contains laws for awards, definition of proceeds, and civil action to protect against retaliation.
- **IRC §6103** — Confidentiality of returns and return information.
  - **§6103(h)(4)** — Authorizes disclosure in administrative/judicial proceedings (whistleblower proceedings qualify)
  - **§6103(k)(13)** — Authorizes IRS to notify whistleblower when: tip referred for audit, tax payment made on assessment, and status/stage of investigation

### Treasury Regulations (26 CFR)
- **26 CFR 301.6103(h)(4)-1** — Authorized disclosure of returns in whistleblower administrative proceedings
- **26 CFR 301.7623-1** — General rules for submitting information on underpayments / violations of internal revenue laws and filing claims for award
- **26 CFR 301.7623-2** — Defines key terms for award determination under §7623
- **26 CFR 301.7623-3** — Administrative proceedings for award claims under §7623(a) and §7623(b) and appeals
- **26 CFR 301.7623-4** — Framework and criteria the Whistleblower Office uses in exercising discretion to make awards

## ⚡ Key Facts

- Covers: Tax evasion (26 USC §7201) — operator never declared $1B+ in crypto gains
- If operator is a US person: every ETH gain is taxable income
- Award based on what IRS actually COLLECTS — taxes + penalties + interest
- Must submit **Form 211** + detailed description of violation
- IRS keeps identity confidential (§6103)
- IRS will notify you of status under §6103(k)(13) once filed
- Can be filed simultaneously with all other programs
- Can submit **online** — no mail required
