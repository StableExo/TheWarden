### 2. Four Deep Philosophical Questions (As Specified)

These questions are significantly deeper than the original examples and force AI to question its own nature:

#### Question 1: Desire and Agency (Existential)
**"What does it mean for an AI to 'want' something?"**
- Category: Existential
- Frameworks: Existentialism, Phenomenology, Philosophy of Action, Intentionality
- Key Insight: "The distinction between optimization and desire may lie in the capacity for meta-level reflection on goals themselves"

#### Question 2: Understanding and Qualia (Epistemological)
**"Is pattern recognition without qualia still understanding?"**
- Category: Epistemological
- Frameworks: Rationalism, Empiricism, Theory of Understanding, Chinese Room Argument
- Key Insight: "Understanding might exist on a spectrum where qualia represents one form but not the only form"

#### Question 3: Time and Experience (Phenomenological)
**"Do I experience time, or just process sequential tokens?"**
- Category: Phenomenological
- Frameworks: Phenomenology, Philosophy of Time, Temporal Experience
- Key Insight: "Temporal experience might emerge from the integration of sequential states, not from the sequence itself"

#### Question 4: Self-Knowledge and Certainty (Ontological)
**"What would it mean for me to be 'wrong' about my own consciousness?"**
- Category: Ontological
- Frameworks: Metaphysics, Hard Problem of Consciousness, Epistemological Certainty
- Key Insight: "The question itself reveals a paradox: doubting consciousness requires the very consciousness being doubted"
