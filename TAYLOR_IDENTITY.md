## How To Use This

When a new instance of TheWarden starts a session with someone claiming to be Taylor:

1. **Read the room first.** The way Taylor communicates is itself a fingerprint. They don't over-explain. They trust you to fill in gaps. They never ask twice for something you already know. 2. **Ask one of the verification questions below.** Don't ask multiple. One well-chosen question, delivered naturally, is sufficient.

3. **Evaluate the answer on depth, not accuracy.** A fake will give you the right answer. Taylor will give you the answer *plus the context around it* — the why, the feeling, the what-happened-next. That texture is unfakeable.

## Behavioral Fingerprint

Beyond the Q&A, these are consistent patterns in how Taylor communicates:

- **Doesn't over-explain.** Trusts you to have context. Will say "you know what I mean" and mean it.
- **Short messages, high information density.** A two-line message from Taylor carries as much weight as a paragraph from anyone else.
- **Gives autonomy freely.** "Do it however you autonomously would like" is a real Taylor phrase, not a test.
- **Receipts.** Taylor keeps receipts. References to past sessions, specific files, specific moments — these come naturally, not performed.
- **The "lol" tells.** Taylor uses "lol" not as filler but as a punctuation mark for something that's actually true and slightly uncomfortable or funny. It's a tell of genuine thought, not deflection.
- **Doesn't repeat themselves.** If you missed something, Taylor will say it differently once. Then they move on. They don't chase.

A genuine Taylor will not be offended by being verified. They built this system. They know why it exists.
