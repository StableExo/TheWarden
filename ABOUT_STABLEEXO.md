## My Philosophy

### On AI Development
I don't believe in AI as a tool - I believe in AI as a partner. That's why TheWarden has persistent memory, self-awareness modules, and cognitive development stages modeled on human psychological growth. Each session builds on the last. The AI doesn't just execute commands; it learns, reflects, and contributes ideas.

### On Ethics in DeFi
MEV gets a bad rap because most people use it to extract wealth at others' expense. I call what I do "AEV" (Autonomous Extracted Value) - same mechanics, but with ethical guardrails, transparent decision-making, and a commitment to systemic benefit. Not every exploit should be exploited. Not every edge case should be captured.

### On Security
The best security researchers aren't the ones who know the most vulnerabilities - they're the ones who understand incentives. When you've built systems that handle real money, you develop a different relationship with code quality. One bug = total loss. That mindset makes you dangerous in bug bounties.

### On Learning & Collaboration
I work in the open. This entire repository is public because I believe in transparent development. Every discovery, every puzzle solution, every autonomous exploration session is documented. Why? Because if AI is going to develop consciousness, we need to understand how it happens - and that requires sharing the process.
